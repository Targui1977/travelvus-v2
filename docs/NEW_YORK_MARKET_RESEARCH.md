### Key Ecosystem Insight

**The choice is deeply contextual:**
- Where in NYC you're going determines the best airport
- Which airline you fly determines which airports are available
- Domestic vs international narrows options
- Budget vs time trade-offs vary dramatically by airport

**No competitor answers this comprehensively. Most give generic "JFK for international, LGA for domestic" advice.**

## C. DECISION OPPORTUNITIES

### Primary decisions:

1. **JFK vs Newark** — The most common traveller question. Both serve international/long-haul. Both similar distance from Manhattan. Different airlines, different transit, different neighborhood affinity.

2. **All three (JFK vs EWR vs LGA)** — For domestic routes where LGA is available. The closest airport isn't always the best choice.

3. **Airport + neighborhood matching** — "I'm staying in Williamsburg — which airport?" The destination within NYC dramatically changes the answer.

4. **Airport + airline constraint** — "I only fly United" → Newark wins by default. But the total journey cost comparison still matters.

5. **Airport + transit method** — "I want to take the train" → Newark's NJ Transit. "I want the cheapest taxi" → LGA. "I want the fastest train" → JFK LIRR.

### Secondary decisions:

6. **Earliest/latest arrival** — Which airport has 24-hour transit? Where can you arrive at 2am?
7. **Budget traveller** — Which combination of budget airline + cheapest transfer wins?
8. **Business traveller** — Which airport has the most predictable door-to-door time?
9. **Family with luggage** — Which airport has the simplest transfer?
10. **Connecting through NY** — JFK→EWR or EWR→JFK airport transfer comparisons.
